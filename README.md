# Cellular neighborhoods of interacting tumor, immune and vascular populations shape response to Sacituzumab Govitecan in triple-negative breast cancer

Ting Liu, Jia-Ren Lin, Jacob Geisberg, Bogang Wu, Laura M. Spring, Veerle Bossuyt, Simona Cristea, Rachel Occhiogrosso Abelman, Nayana Thimmiah, Isabella Vianna, Ilze Smidt, Nicole Peiris, James Coates, Siang-Boon Koh, A. C. Garrido-Castro, F. Lynce, N. ## SUMMARY

Antibody drug conjugates (ADCs) are complex therapeutics that have revolutionized breast cancer treatment, yet their clinically relevant mechanisms remain poorly defined. Here, we analyzed pre- and post-treatment specimens from a phase 2 clinical trial of pre-operative single-agent sacituzumab govitecan (SG), a Trophoblast cell surface antigen 2 (TROP2)-targeted ADC delivering a topoisomerase 1 inhibitor payload, in patients with localized Triple-Negative Breast Cancer (TNBC). Using integrated genome, single-cell transcriptome, and multiplex spatial protein profiling, we compared tumors achieving pathologic complete response (pCR) with those exhibiting residual disease (RD). Neither average TROP2 protein expression nor its inter- or intra-cellular distribution was associated with response. Instead, responding tumors displayed proliferative tumor cells and an activated immune landscape enriched in stem-like CD8+ T cells, tertiary lymphoid structures, and immune effector populations consistent with enhanced antibody-dependent cellular cytotoxicity and phagocytosis. By contrast, RD tumors were characterized by tumor cell-intrinsic phenotypes of chronic interferon (IFN) signaling and quiescence, organized spatially into small, immune-poor tumor cell clusters, and surrounded by architecturally disordered, angiogenic endothelium together with an immunosuppressive microenvironment. Collectively, our data suggest that interactions between resistant tumor cell, immune and angiogenic populations define the tumor ecosystems that shape response to SG. This analysis reveals why TROP2 expression alone is a poor predictor of response, and illustrates how a multimodal discovery approach can yield biological insights relevant to clinical biomarkers and therapeutic opportunities. 

## ACCESS THE DATA

Example images can be accessed through Harvard tissue Atlas website . Cell count tables have been released via Github ([https://github.com/labsyspharm/Chen-Santagata-Kidney-2026](https://github.com/Michorlab/NeoSTAR/)). ## Codes for imaging data processing

High-plex whole-slide images were acquired using Orion high-plex immunofluorescence (Orion; Lin et al, 2023) and then segmented and quantified using MCMICRO (https://github.com/labsyspharm/mcmicro).
